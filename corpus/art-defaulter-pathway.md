# ART Missed-Dose & Defaulter Tracing Pathway

## Section 1 — Definitions

- **Missed appointment**: patient did not collect their scheduled refill within 7 days of the due date.
- **Defaulter**: patient has missed appointments for ≥ 28 consecutive days.
- **Lost to follow-up (LTFU)**: patient has missed appointments for ≥ 90 consecutive days with no contact.

These definitions align with WHO ART continuity-of-care guidance. They are tracked in the facility ART register and aggregated to district level.

## Section 2 — Roles and responsibilities

- **Community Health Worker (CHW)**: first-line contact for missed appointments. Conducts initial home visit and re-engagement counseling.
- **Facility ART nurse**: maintains the ART register; confirms defaulter status; logs the defaulter form.
- **Facility supervisor**: receives defaulter forms; coordinates with district ART coordinator for cases LTFU > 90 days.
- **District ART coordinator**: oversees defaulter tracing across all facilities in the district; phone contact during business hours.

## Section 3 — Trigger criteria

The defaulter pathway is initiated when ANY of the following occurs:

1. Patient does not collect a scheduled ART refill within 7 days of the due date.
2. Patient does not attend a scheduled clinical review within 14 days of the appointment.
3. CHW or facility staff observe a community signal suggesting treatment interruption (e.g. family member reports the patient has stopped medication).

If none of the above apply, the patient is not yet in the defaulter pathway.

## Section 4 — Standard procedure

### 4.1 First contact (within 7 days of missed appointment)

CHW or facility-assigned tracer attempts contact in this order:

1. **Phone call** to the registered patient contact number. Single attempt; if no answer, log and proceed.
2. **SMS reminder** sent same day. Standard text: "Your refill at [facility] was due [date]. Please come collect or call your CHW."
3. **No further escalation** until 14 days have elapsed.

Outcome documented in the ART register: `phoned`, `texted`, `reached`, `unreachable`.

### 4.2 Home visit (between days 14 and 28)

If the patient remains unreached at 14 days post-due date, the CHW conducts a home visit:

1. Visit the registered home address during morning hours where possible (better likelihood of contact).
2. If the patient is reached, conduct a brief re-engagement conversation (see Section 5).
3. If the patient is not at home, leave a discreet note (no HIV-specific language) and attempt one repeat visit within the same week.
4. If the patient is reached and refuses to re-engage, do not pressure; document refusal and escalate to facility supervisor.

Document outcome in the defaulter form (paper or digital): `re-engaged`, `refused`, `not located`.

### 4.3 Defaulter declaration (day 28)

If the patient has not collected ART for ≥ 28 days and has not been re-engaged, the facility nurse formally declares defaulter status:

1. Complete the defaulter form with last contact date, attempts made, last known address.
2. File the form with the facility supervisor.
3. Continue monthly tracing attempts for up to 90 days.
4. Patient remains on the active register until LTFU is declared.

### 4.4 Lost to follow-up (day 90)

After 90 days with no contact, the patient is reclassified as LTFU and removed from the active treatment cohort. The district ART coordinator is notified for inclusion in the quarterly LTFU review.

## Section 5 — Re-engagement counseling

When a CHW or facility staff member successfully reaches a patient who has missed doses, the conversation should:

1. **Open without judgment.** Avoid language that implies blame ("Why did you stop?"). Open with: "We noticed you missed your refill — is everything okay?"
2. **Listen for the actual reason.** Common reasons in Lesotho contexts include: travel for work, side effects, stigma in the household, distance to facility, transport cost, missed reminder.
3. **Address the specific barrier.** If transport is the issue, escalate to facility for community ART distribution. If side effects, escalate for clinical review. If stigma, link to peer support group.
4. **Confirm next refill date.** Make the next collection date concrete and offer a reminder mechanism (CHW phone reminder, SMS, family member).
5. **Document the reason for missed doses.** This data feeds the district-level interruption analysis.

## Section 6 — Special cases

### 6.1 Pregnant or breastfeeding women

Defaulter tracing for pregnant or breastfeeding women on ART is **expedited**: home visit at day 7 (not day 14), and facility supervisor is notified immediately. Vertical transmission risk drives the urgency.

### 6.2 Pediatric patients

For patients under 18, the registered guardian is contacted at first attempt. Home visit at day 14 includes the guardian. All escalations go through the facility supervisor; the district pediatric ART focal person is consulted for LTFU determination.

### 6.3 Patients with documented mental health or substance use

Standard timeline applies but psychosocial support team is engaged at day 14 alongside the CHW visit. Home visits are conducted in pairs.

## Section 7 — When to escalate

Escalate immediately to the **District ART Coordinator** when:

- Patient appears in critical condition during home visit
- Patient discloses suicidal ideation or active self-harm
- Patient discloses gender-based violence as a barrier to adherence
- Patient is pediatric and guardian is unreachable for ≥ 14 days
- More than 5 active defaulters identified in a single facility within one week (suggests systemic issue)

## Section 8 — Data and reporting

CHW and facility staff report defaulter activity weekly via the standard form. Aggregated district-level data is reported to MoH monthly. Indicators tracked:

- Number of patients with missed appointments (7-day, 14-day, 28-day)
- Number of successful re-engagements
- Reasons for missed doses (top 3 by district)
- Defaulter rate by facility (denominator: active ART cohort)

These data feed the quarterly continuity-of-care review at MoH.
