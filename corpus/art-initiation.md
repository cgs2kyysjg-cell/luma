# ART Initiation Protocol

## Section 1 — Definitions

- **Treat All**: WHO and Lesotho policy that all people with confirmed HIV are eligible for ART, regardless of CD4 count or clinical stage.
- **Same-day ART initiation**: starting ART on the same day as a confirmed HIV-positive diagnosis. Recommended for clinically stable clients without contraindications.
- **Rapid initiation**: ART started within 7 days of diagnosis when same-day is not possible.
- **First-line regimen**: the standard initial ART combination per national guidance. In Lesotho and most SSA contexts, this is currently a dolutegravir-based combination (e.g., TLD: tenofovir + lamivudine + dolutegravir).
- **Viral suppression**: viral load below the laboratory threshold of detection (typically <50 or <1000 copies/mL depending on assay). Goal of treatment.

## Section 2 — Roles and responsibilities

- **CHW**: post-test counseling for clients newly confirmed positive; linkage support to facility; adherence counseling at month-1 follow-up; tracing if client misses initiation.
- **Facility ART nurse / clinician**: clinical assessment; baseline labs; ART prescription and initiation; first-month review.
- **Lab technician**: baseline CD4 (where available), viral load at month 6, full blood count, creatinine.
- **District ART coordinator**: oversight of initiation cascades, drug supply, MDR-HIV referral.

CHWs do not prescribe ART or adjust regimens. They support the linkage and adherence pathway.

## Section 3 — Eligibility and timing

Following confirmed HIV-positive diagnosis (per HTS protocol), every client is **eligible** for ART under Treat-All. Initiation timing depends on clinical stability:

### 3.1 Same-day initiation (preferred)

Offer same-day ART if all of the following are true:

1. The client understands they are HIV-positive and consents to start ART.
2. The client has no signs of severe acute illness (fever, vomiting, severe weight loss, altered mental status).
3. The client has no signs of active TB symptoms (cough ≥ 2 weeks, night sweats, unexplained weight loss). If symptoms present, screen for TB before ART per Section 4.
4. The client is not pregnant with active opportunistic infection requiring management first.
5. Drug supply is available at the facility.

Same-day initiation reduces loss-to-follow-up between diagnosis and treatment significantly.

### 3.2 Deferred initiation (within 7 days)

Defer initiation only if:

- Client requires evaluation for active TB or other opportunistic infection that affects regimen choice.
- Client is not yet psychologically ready and has explicitly requested time. Schedule follow-up within 7 days.
- Specific drug-drug interaction concerns require clinician review.

If deferred, the CHW conducts a follow-up contact within 72 hours to confirm the facility appointment is kept.

## Section 4 — Pre-initiation steps (facility)

The facility nurse or clinician performs the following before prescribing ART:

1. **TB screening**: ask about cough, fever, night sweats, weight loss. If any positive, refer to TB workup before initiating ART (see TB Treatment Protocol).
2. **Pregnancy status**: confirm pregnancy status for women of reproductive age. Pregnant clients follow PMTCT pathway; the regimen and follow-up are similar but with PMTCT-specific monitoring.
3. **Co-infection screen**: hepatitis B, syphilis. Hepatitis B status affects the regimen — tenofovir-containing regimens are preferred for co-infected patients.
4. **Baseline labs** where available: CD4, full blood count, creatinine. Lab access varies in Lesotho; do not delay ART initiation while waiting for non-urgent results unless severe abnormalities are suspected.
5. **Symptom and weight check**: document baseline weight; ART dosing follows national weight bands.
6. **Adherence counseling**: 15–20 minutes covering daily-dose commitment, side effects, what to do if a dose is missed, partner disclosure plans, ARV pickup schedule.

The CHW supports steps 5 and 6 where they have capacity.

## Section 5 — First-line ART regimen (overview)

The current first-line regimen in most SSA programmes including Lesotho is a **dolutegravir-based** fixed-dose combination, typically **TLD (tenofovir + lamivudine + dolutegravir)** taken as one pill once daily.

CHWs do not prescribe or dose. They reinforce the regimen the facility has prescribed:

1. **Take exactly one tablet, once a day, every day, at roughly the same time.**
2. **Take with food** (helps reduce nausea in the first weeks).
3. **Don't share the medication.** It's prescribed for one client only.
4. **If a dose is missed**: take it as soon as remembered if within 12 hours of the missed time. If more than 12 hours, skip and take the next dose at the normal time. Don't double up.
5. **Side effects**: mild headache, sleep changes, or nausea are common in the first 2–4 weeks. Report severe rash, jaundice, or persistent vomiting to the facility immediately.

## Section 6 — Follow-up schedule

The standard follow-up cadence after ART initiation:

| Visit | Timing | Who | What |
|---|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 4 weeks after initiation | CHW or facility | Adherence check, side effect screen, weight |
| Month 3 | 12 weeks | Facility | Clinical review, refill |
| Month 6 | 24 weeks | Facility | First viral load test, clinical review |
| Every 3–6 months thereafter | per stability | Facility | Refill, clinical review |
| Annually | every 12 months | Facility | Repeat viral load |

Stable clients (suppressed viral load, no clinical issues) may transition to Multi-Month Dispensing (MMD) — receiving 3 or 6 months of ARVs per pickup.

## Section 7 — Special groups

### 7.1 Pregnant women

Same-day ART initiation is the standard. The regimen is the same first-line combination unless there's a specific contraindication. PMTCT-specific follow-up applies (see PMTCT protocol).

### 7.2 Adolescents (10–19 years)

Adolescents face higher rates of treatment interruption. Initiation should include a peer-support linkage where possible. Adolescent-friendly clinic hours and reminders improve outcomes. Confidentiality from parents must be respected per the adolescent's wishes.

### 7.3 People with active TB

Active TB must be evaluated and TB treatment initiated. ART initiation is then timed based on CD4:
- Severely immunosuppressed (CD4 < 50): ART within 2 weeks of starting TB treatment.
- All others: ART within 8 weeks of starting TB treatment.

These decisions are made by the facility clinician. CHWs reinforce the dual-treatment regimen.

### 7.4 Clients with known mental health issues or substance use

Initiation is not delayed, but psychosocial support is layered: peer support group referral, mental health screening, harm reduction support if indicated.

## Section 8 — When to escalate

Escalate immediately to the **District ART Coordinator** when:

- Client is unable to be initiated within 7 days due to drug stockout
- Client presents with severe rash, jaundice, or vision changes after starting ART (suspected drug toxicity)
- Client at month 6 has a viral load result NOT showing suppression — referral for adherence intensification or possible regimen switch
- Pediatric (under 18 months) HIV-positive client confirmed via DNA PCR — requires pediatric ART specialist
- Pregnant client at any gestational age unable to start ART within 48 hours of diagnosis

## Section 9 — Data and reporting

Facility-level ART initiation indicators reported monthly:

- Number of clients newly diagnosed HIV-positive
- Number initiated on ART within 7 days of diagnosis (and same-day subset)
- Number lost to follow-up between diagnosis and initiation
- Number with month-6 viral load result available
- Number with month-6 viral load suppressed

These data feed the quarterly HIV programme review at MoH.
