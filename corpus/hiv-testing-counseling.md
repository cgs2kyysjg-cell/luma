# HIV Testing & Counseling (HTS)

## Section 1 — Definitions and modalities

- **HTS (HIV Testing Services)**: the full pathway from offering a test, through counseling, testing, result delivery, and linkage to care.
- **Provider-initiated testing and counseling (PITC)**: HIV test offered by a health worker as part of a routine clinical encounter.
- **Client-initiated testing and counseling (CITC)**: client requests an HIV test on their own initiative.
- **Index testing**: HIV-positive client's sexual or needle-sharing partners and biological children are offered testing.
- **HIV self-testing (HIVST)**: client uses an oral-fluid or blood-based self-test kit independently. The CHW role is distribution, instruction, and linkage of reactive results.
- **Reactive (rapid test)**: a single rapid test came back positive. Confirmation is required before declaring HIV-positive status.
- **Confirmed HIV-positive**: at least two different rapid tests in sequence are reactive (the national testing algorithm), OR a laboratory-confirmed result.

## Section 2 — Roles and responsibilities

- **Community Health Worker (CHW)**: distributes HIV self-test kits in the community; offers PITC during home visits where trained; provides pre- and post-test counseling under supervision; links reactive cases to facility for confirmation.
- **Facility nurse / counselor**: conducts the full national testing algorithm; delivers confirmed results; initiates ART or referral.
- **District HIV programme coordinator**: oversees HTS programme indicators; manages testing kit supply; reviews stockouts.

CHWs must complete an approved HTS training before independently offering provider-initiated tests. CHWs do not interpret confirmation results — they refer reactive cases to the facility.

## Section 3 — When to offer HIV testing

Offer testing routinely (provider-initiated) in any of the following situations:

1. **First antenatal care visit** — every pregnant woman, every pregnancy.
2. **Re-testing in pregnancy** — at 36 weeks and again 6 weeks postpartum, regardless of prior result.
3. **Active or presumptive TB** — every person screened for TB.
4. **STI presentation** — every person presenting with an STI symptom.
5. **Index contact** — every named partner or biological child of a confirmed HIV-positive client.
6. **High-risk groups** — people who inject drugs, sex workers, gay men and other men who have sex with men, transgender people. Offer at every contact.
7. **Routine annual testing** — for adults in high-prevalence settings (Lesotho qualifies). Once per year for the general population aged 15+.
8. **On request (CITC)** — anyone requesting a test, at any time, without requiring a justification.

Offer testing as **opt-out** in routine care: explain that testing is part of standard care unless they decline. Always document the offer and the response.

## Section 4 — Pre-test counseling (3–5 minutes)

Before any HIV test, every client receives:

1. **Why offer**: explain why HIV testing is being offered (e.g., "Testing is part of routine antenatal care").
2. **What the test does**: explains a rapid finger-prick test detects HIV antibodies; results in 15–20 minutes; if reactive, a confirmatory test is done before any diagnosis is given.
3. **Window period**: if the client may have been exposed in the last 6 weeks, explain the test may not yet detect a recent infection; offer re-testing in 6 weeks.
4. **Confidentiality**: results are shared only with the client unless they choose to involve a partner.
5. **What happens if reactive**: explain the linkage pathway — confirmation, baseline labs, and ART initiation if confirmed positive. ART is highly effective; viral suppression means undetectable = untransmissible (U=U).
6. **What happens if non-reactive**: re-testing recommendations based on risk; prevention options including PrEP for high-risk individuals.
7. **Consent**: confirm verbal consent before drawing blood. Document consent in the HTS register.

Skip rote scripts. Tailor the message to the client's stated reason for testing or visit.

## Section 5 — Test procedure

CHWs follow the national testing algorithm — typically two rapid tests in sequence:

1. **First test**: rapid HIV test (finger-prick or oral). Read at the manufacturer-specified time (usually 15–20 minutes).
2. **If non-reactive**: result is negative for current testing. Provide post-test counseling with prevention messages.
3. **If reactive**: do NOT give the client an HIV-positive result yet. Conduct the second rapid test (different brand) per the national algorithm. If the second test is also reactive, the result is treated as confirmed positive per national guidance — link to facility for any laboratory confirmation if required.
4. **If discordant** (first reactive, second non-reactive): the client is **not** HIV-positive based on this testing. Refer to facility for laboratory confirmation; do not declare a status.

Document every test result on the HTS register and on the client's confidential record.

## Section 6 — Post-test counseling

### 6.1 Negative result

1. Affirm the result and confirm the window-period caveat if relevant.
2. Provide prevention messages tailored to the client's risk profile: condom use, PrEP eligibility, partner testing, STI screening.
3. Recommend re-testing intervals: every 12 months for general population, every 3–6 months for high-risk groups.

### 6.2 Reactive / confirmed positive result

1. Deliver the result clearly and without euphemism: "Your HIV test result is positive."
2. Pause. Allow the client time to react. Do not rush.
3. Confirm understanding: "What does this result mean to you?"
4. Reassure: HIV is treatable; ART makes the virus undetectable; people on ART live long, healthy lives.
5. **Same-day linkage**: where possible, link to ART initiation the same day. See ART Initiation protocol.
6. **Disclosure planning**: discuss who the client wants to tell and when. Do NOT pressure disclosure.
7. **Index testing offer**: ask about sexual partners and biological children who may benefit from testing. Index testing is voluntary and confidential.
8. **Psychosocial support**: link to peer support, mental health screening if indicated, GBV screening if indicated.

If the client refuses immediate linkage, schedule a follow-up within 7 days. Do not lose the client to follow-up at this stage.

## Section 7 — Special populations

### 7.1 Pregnant women

Confirmed HIV-positive pregnant women are **immediate priority**: same-day ART initiation, expedited PMTCT pathway. See PMTCT protocol.

### 7.2 Adolescents (10–19 years)

Adolescents may receive HIV testing without parental consent in many contexts; confirm the current Lesotho legal age of consent for HIV testing with the district HIV coordinator. Test in a private, judgment-free setting. Avoid involving parents unless the adolescent requests it. Link reactive adolescents to age-appropriate ART services.

### 7.3 Index testing — partner notification

Index testing is **voluntary**. Three approaches exist:

1. **Client referral**: index client tells their partners themselves and brings them to test.
2. **Provider referral**: with index client's explicit permission, the health worker contacts named partners.
3. **Contract referral**: a hybrid where the client agrees to bring the partner within a specified window; if not, the health worker makes contact.

Never disclose the index client's HIV status to the partner without explicit consent. Screen for intimate partner violence risk before any partner notification — if IPV risk is present, do NOT proceed with partner notification.

### 7.4 HIV self-test (HIVST) follow-up

If a client uses a self-test kit and gets a reactive result, the kit instructions and CHW counseling must direct them to the facility for confirmatory testing. CHWs distributing kits must record distribution and follow up at 14 days to confirm whether the client tested and what the outcome was.

## Section 8 — When to escalate

Escalate immediately to the **District HIV Programme Coordinator** when:

- A reactive result occurs in a client with a history of severe mental health crisis or suicidal ideation
- Index testing reveals suspected GBV that requires safe-house referral
- A pediatric (under 18 months) reactive result is obtained — these require infant-specific virological testing (DNA PCR), not antibody testing
- Stockout of testing kits at the facility for more than 48 hours
- Clustering of reactive results in a single household or community group (suggests local transmission event)

## Section 9 — Data and reporting

CHW and facility HTS indicators reported monthly:

- Number of HTS encounters offered (by entry point: PITC, CITC, index, HIVST)
- Number of clients tested
- Number of reactive results
- Number of reactive results linked to confirmation within 48 hours
- Number of confirmed HIV-positive clients linked to ART within 7 days
- Index testing uptake rate (named partners offered : tested : reactive)

These data feed the quarterly HIV programme review at MoH.
