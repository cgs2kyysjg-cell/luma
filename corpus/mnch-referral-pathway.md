# Maternal, Newborn & Child Health Referral Pathway

## Section 1 — Antenatal danger signs (mother)

The following symptoms in a pregnant woman are **immediate referral** indications. CHW or facility staff should not attempt management at the village or peripheral level:

1. **Severe headache with blurred vision** — possible pre-eclampsia
2. **Convulsions or fits** — possible eclampsia (life-threatening)
3. **Vaginal bleeding** at any gestational age
4. **Severe abdominal pain** unrelieved by rest
5. **Reduced or absent fetal movements** after 28 weeks
6. **Fever ≥ 38°C** with chills
7. **Severe pallor or shortness of breath at rest**
8. **Swelling of face and hands** (not just feet)

Action: arrange immediate transport to the nearest health facility with comprehensive emergency obstetric care (CEmOC). Do not delay for additional assessment.

## Section 2 — Routine antenatal contacts

Standard MoH guidance is **8 antenatal contacts** across pregnancy:

| Contact | Timing |
|---|---|
| 1 | Before 12 weeks |
| 2 | 20 weeks |
| 3 | 26 weeks |
| 4 | 30 weeks |
| 5 | 34 weeks |
| 6 | 36 weeks |
| 7 | 38 weeks |
| 8 | 40 weeks |

CHW role at each contact:

1. Confirm the woman has attended her facility-based ANC visit.
2. Screen for danger signs (Section 1).
3. Reinforce birth preparedness messaging: planned facility, transport, support person, items packed.
4. Confirm HIV status and ART continuity if known positive (cross-reference ART defaulter pathway).
5. Document the contact on the woman's ANC card.

## Section 3 — Intrapartum referral

The following are reasons for immediate intrapartum referral from a peripheral facility or planned home birth to a CEmOC facility:

1. **Prolonged labor** (active phase > 12 hours for primigravida, > 8 hours for multigravida)
2. **Obstructed labor** (no progress despite adequate contractions)
3. **Cord prolapse**
4. **Antepartum or intrapartum hemorrhage**
5. **Eclampsia or pre-eclampsia symptoms** in labor
6. **Suspected uterine rupture** (severe abdominal pain, shock, fetal distress)
7. **Malpresentation** detected late in labor (breech, transverse)
8. **Severe fetal distress** (heart rate < 100 or > 180, persistent)

Action: stabilize where possible, arrange urgent transport, communicate ahead to the receiving facility, accompany the patient.

## Section 4 — Postnatal danger signs

### 4.1 Mother (within 6 weeks postpartum)

Refer immediately if any of:

1. Heavy vaginal bleeding (soaking pads more than 1 per hour)
2. Foul-smelling vaginal discharge
3. Fever ≥ 38°C
4. Severe headache or visual disturbance
5. Severe perineal or abdominal pain
6. Calf pain or swelling (suspected DVT)
7. Symptoms of postpartum depression or psychosis (suicidal ideation, hallucinations, inability to care for newborn) — refer to facility mental health support

### 4.2 Newborn (first 28 days)

Refer immediately if any of:

1. Difficulty breathing or fast breathing (> 60/min)
2. Severe chest indrawing
3. Convulsions or jitteriness
4. Body too cold (< 35.5°C) or too hot (> 37.5°C)
5. Yellow palms and soles (severe jaundice)
6. Not feeding or weak suck
7. Reduced movement, lethargy
8. Persistent vomiting
9. Cord stump red, swollen, or with discharge

### 4.3 Routine postnatal contacts

Six routine PNC contacts:

| Contact | Timing |
|---|---|
| 1 | Within 24 hours of birth |
| 2 | Day 3 |
| 3 | Day 7 |
| 4 | Week 2 |
| 5 | Week 6 |
| 6 | Week 12 |

CHW conducts community-based PNC contacts; facility nurse conducts the day-3 and week-6 visits.

## Section 5 — Child health referral (under 5)

### 5.1 IMCI danger signs (immediate referral)

Use the IMCI (Integrated Management of Childhood Illness) danger sign assessment for any sick child under 5:

1. **Unable to drink or breastfeed**
2. **Vomits everything**
3. **Convulsions** (now or in this illness)
4. **Lethargic or unconscious**
5. **Severe acute malnutrition** (visible wasting, bilateral pitting edema)

Any one sign present = immediate referral to facility.

### 5.2 Specific symptom pathways

- **Cough or difficulty breathing**: count breaths over 1 minute. Fast breathing thresholds — under 2 months: ≥ 60; 2 months to 1 year: ≥ 50; 1 year to 5 years: ≥ 40. Severe pneumonia signs (chest indrawing, stridor): refer immediately.
- **Diarrhea**: assess hydration. Severe dehydration (sunken eyes, slow skin pinch return, lethargic) requires immediate referral. Mild-to-moderate: ORS at home with reassessment in 24 hours.
- **Fever**: in malaria-endemic seasons, refer for RDT (rapid diagnostic test) at facility. Fever > 7 days = refer regardless.

### 5.3 Immunization

CHW role: identify children with missed immunizations, refer to facility for catch-up. Standard schedule (BCG, OPV, pentavalent, PCV, measles, etc.) follows the national EPI calendar.

## Section 6 — Special groups

### 6.1 Adolescent pregnancy (< 20 years)

Adolescent pregnancies receive:

1. Earlier and more frequent ANC contacts (consider adding contacts at 16 and 24 weeks).
2. Mandatory facility-based delivery — do not plan for home delivery.
3. Social work referral for psychosocial support and continuity of education planning.
4. Screening for IPV (intimate partner violence) at every contact.

### 6.2 HIV-positive pregnant women

Cross-reference the ART defaulter pathway. Vertical transmission risk drives heightened vigilance:

1. ANC at every contact must confirm ART continuity.
2. Missed ART refill in pregnancy = home visit at day 7 (not day 14).
3. Newborn receives prophylaxis per national PMTCT guidance.

### 6.3 High-altitude considerations

Many Lesotho districts are at altitudes > 2,000m. Pregnancies at high altitude have elevated risk of intrauterine growth restriction. Where altitude is a known factor, document maternal weight gain trajectory more closely and refer for facility ultrasound at 28 and 34 weeks.

## Section 7 — When to escalate

Escalate immediately to the **District MNCH Coordinator** when:

- Maternal death or near-miss in the catchment area
- Newborn death within 28 days
- Child death under 5 at home
- Cluster of 3+ children with severe acute malnutrition in one village
- Adolescent pregnancy where IPV is suspected
- Pattern of missed ANC visits across multiple women in the same village (suggests systemic barrier)

## Section 8 — Data and reporting

Facility and CHW MNCH indicators reported monthly:

- Number of women receiving ANC contact 1 / 4 / 8
- Number of facility deliveries
- Number of postnatal contacts within 48 hours
- Maternal and perinatal mortality cases
- Under-5 mortality cases
- Children with severe acute malnutrition identified
- Immunization coverage

These data feed quarterly district MNCH reviews.
