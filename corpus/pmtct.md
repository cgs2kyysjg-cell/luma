# PMTCT — Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission

## Section 1 — Definitions

- **PMTCT (Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission)**: the package of interventions that minimizes the risk of HIV transmission from a pregnant or breastfeeding mother to her infant. Vertical transmission risk drops from ~30% (without intervention) to <2% with full PMTCT.
- **Option B+**: lifelong ART for every HIV-positive pregnant woman, started at diagnosis, continued for life. The current standard in Lesotho and most SSA programmes.
- **Infant prophylaxis**: ARV prophylaxis given to the HIV-exposed infant for a defined period after birth. Drug and duration depend on maternal viral load and breastfeeding status.
- **HIV-exposed infant (HEI)**: an infant born to a known or suspected HIV-positive mother. Followed in dedicated HEI clinics from birth to final HIV status determination (typically 18 months or end of breastfeeding).
- **Final HIV status**: confirmed by HIV antibody testing at 18 months OR 6 weeks after cessation of breastfeeding, whichever is later.

## Section 2 — Roles and responsibilities

- **CHW**: identifies pregnant women in the community; supports linkage to ANC; provides adherence support to HIV-positive mothers; conducts postnatal home visits including HEI follow-up reminders.
- **ANC nurse / midwife**: HIV testing at first ANC and re-testing at 36 weeks and 6 weeks postpartum; same-day ART initiation for newly diagnosed; PMTCT-specific monitoring.
- **Maternity / labor ward staff**: confirm maternal ART continuity at admission; administer infant prophylaxis at birth.
- **HEI clinic / facility**: schedules infant HIV testing (DNA PCR at 6 weeks, 9 months, 18 months); coordinates with pediatric ART if infant is positive.
- **District PMTCT focal person**: oversight of PMTCT cascade indicators.

## Section 3 — The PMTCT cascade

PMTCT is monitored as a cascade of steps. Loss at any step increases vertical transmission risk:

1. **All pregnant women HIV-tested at first ANC contact**
2. **All HIV-positive pregnant women initiated on ART**
3. **All HIV-positive pregnant women retained on ART through delivery**
4. **All HIV-exposed infants given infant prophylaxis**
5. **All HIV-exposed infants tested with DNA PCR at 6 weeks**
6. **All HIV-positive infants linked to pediatric ART within 14 days of result**
7. **Final HIV status determined at 18 months for all HEIs**

CHW work focuses on steps 1, 2, 3, 5, and 7 — the community-facing steps.

## Section 4 — Antenatal pathway

### 4.1 First ANC contact

- HIV testing offered to every pregnant woman per HTS protocol — opt-out.
- If HIV-positive (newly diagnosed or known): same-day ART initiation. Regimen is the same first-line combination unless contraindicated (see ART Initiation protocol).
- If HIV-negative: counsel on PrEP eligibility (see PrEP protocol) — pregnancy doesn't reduce HIV risk if exposure is ongoing.
- Document HIV status, ART start date, and baseline labs in the ANC card.

### 4.2 Re-testing at 36 weeks

Every previously HIV-negative pregnant woman is re-tested at 36 weeks (or whenever the third trimester ANC contact occurs). Late maternal seroconversion is a significant transmission risk.

### 4.3 Pregnancy on ART

Pregnant women on ART receive standard ANC plus:

1. **Adherence reinforcement at every contact**: missed doses in pregnancy = high transmission risk. CHW conducts home visits at any sign of interruption (per ART Defaulter pathway, with expedited timeline — home visit at day 7, not day 14).
2. **Viral load monitoring**: at first ANC contact (or earliest pregnancy contact) and again in late pregnancy (around 32–36 weeks). The latter result drives delivery and infant-prophylaxis planning.
3. **Disclosure support**: many women face partner disclosure challenges in pregnancy. Offer assisted partner notification per HTS § 7.3 if requested.
4. **Family planning counseling for postpartum**: planning is offered in third trimester so a method is in place at delivery.

### 4.4 If maternal viral load is unsuppressed in late pregnancy

Maternal viral load >1000 copies/mL near delivery indicates higher transmission risk. The facility clinician:

- Intensifies adherence support
- Considers regimen review
- Plans infant prophylaxis on the higher-risk pathway (longer duration, dual-drug regimen per national guidance)

This is a clinician decision. CHW role is to support the mother through the additional follow-up.

## Section 5 — Intrapartum and immediate postnatal

### 5.1 Delivery

All women living with HIV should deliver at a health facility, not at home. CHW counsels on the importance of facility delivery throughout pregnancy and supports transport planning.

At delivery, the labor staff:

- Confirm maternal ART continuity (verify last dose taken)
- Avoid procedures that increase transmission risk where possible (artificial rupture of membranes only if clearly indicated, etc.)
- Document delivery details in the maternal record
- Initiate infant prophylaxis within 6–12 hours of birth

### 5.2 Infant prophylaxis at birth

The HIV-exposed infant receives ARV prophylaxis starting within 6–12 hours of birth. Drug, dose, and duration are clinician decisions per national guidance — typically 6 weeks of nevirapine for low-risk exposures or longer dual-drug regimens for higher-risk exposures.

CHWs do not dose. They reinforce the schedule the facility has set and screen for adherence at home visits.

## Section 6 — Postnatal and infant follow-up

### 6.1 Postnatal visits

CHW conducts postnatal home visits at standard MNCH cadence (24 hours, day 3, day 7, week 2, week 6, week 12 — see MNCH protocol). At each visit specifically for HIV-exposed infants:

1. Confirm infant prophylaxis is being given
2. Confirm maternal ART continuity
3. Screen mother for postpartum depression (heightened concern in HIV-positive new mothers)
4. Reinforce breastfeeding guidance — exclusive breastfeeding for first 6 months; mixed feeding is NOT recommended (mixed feeding has higher transmission risk than exclusive breastfeeding alone)
5. Confirm 6-week DNA PCR appointment is scheduled and kept

### 6.2 Infant HIV testing schedule

Standard testing schedule for the HIV-exposed infant:

| Test | Timing | Method |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 weeks | DNA PCR (NOT antibody — maternal antibodies cross the placenta and persist up to 18 months) |
| 2 | 9 months | DNA PCR |
| 3 | 18 months OR 6 weeks after end of breastfeeding | HIV antibody test (final status determination) |

A reactive DNA PCR result in an infant requires immediate confirmatory testing and pediatric ART initiation if confirmed positive — typically within 14 days.

### 6.3 Breastfeeding guidance

Exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months, then continued breastfeeding alongside complementary feeding for at least 12 months total, with cessation typically by 24 months.

Mothers must remain on ART throughout breastfeeding. Stopping ART during breastfeeding sharply increases transmission risk.

## Section 7 — Special situations

### 7.1 Mother newly diagnosed HIV-positive in labor

If a woman tests HIV-positive for the first time during labor:

1. Offer same-day ART per ART Initiation protocol
2. Initiate higher-risk infant prophylaxis (longer duration, dual-drug regimen) per facility clinician decision
3. Schedule mother and infant for facility-based PMTCT follow-up at 1 week
4. Assist disclosure planning post-delivery — never disclose to partner without consent

### 7.2 Adolescent pregnancy (HIV-positive)

Combine adolescent-friendly ART services (see ART Initiation § 7.2) with PMTCT-specific support. Higher rates of treatment interruption mean more frequent CHW contact (every 2 weeks through pregnancy is reasonable).

### 7.3 Mother with prior PMTCT failure

A mother who had a previous HIV-positive infant outcome despite PMTCT requires intensified support: review of prior adherence patterns, address barriers, consider direct observation of doses. Refer to facility for clinician review of regimen.

### 7.4 Discordant couples

In a relationship where one partner is HIV-positive on ART and the other is negative, PrEP for the negative partner is recommended (see PrEP protocol). Conception planning may include condomless sex during the fertile window if the positive partner has documented viral suppression for at least 6 months (U=U).

## Section 8 — When to escalate

Escalate immediately to the **District PMTCT Focal Person** when:

- Mother diagnosed HIV-positive in labor
- Mother on ART with viral load > 1000 copies/mL in late pregnancy
- HIV-exposed infant misses 6-week DNA PCR
- HIV-exposed infant has confirmed positive DNA PCR at any time point — pediatric ART required within 14 days
- Mother defaulting from ART during pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Infant prophylaxis stockout at facility for more than 24 hours

## Section 9 — Data and reporting

PMTCT cascade indicators reported monthly:

- Number of pregnant women attending first ANC
- Number HIV-tested at first ANC
- Number HIV-positive (known + new)
- Number initiated on ART
- Number with viral load done in third trimester
- Number HIV-exposed infants delivered
- Number of HEIs receiving prophylaxis
- Number of HEIs with 6-week DNA PCR done
- Number of HEIs with confirmed final HIV status at 18 months
- Mother-to-child transmission rate (calculated quarterly)

These data feed the quarterly PMTCT review at MoH.
